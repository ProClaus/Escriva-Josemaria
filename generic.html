“Every idea begins as a sketch; I turn mine into impact.” Escriva.

Escriva is a Public Health professional, program manager, researcher, humanitarian, and mental health advocate. He studied at the Catholic University of Eastern Africa and has since built a career designing and implementing programs that improve access to preventive care, empower young people, and strengthen community resilience. Escriva’s work spans community health, humanitarian response, youth mentorship, and academic research support. He has contributed to wide-reaching health campaigns that impacted thousands, coordinated outreach initiatives, and guided students and emerging professionals in research, data analysis, and program development. As a research assistant and program manager, Escriva has developed expertise in tools such as SPSS, DHIS2, and advanced data visualization platforms, harnessing technology to bridge the gap between data and human experience. He is recognized for his ability to translate complex information into human-centered insights that drive action, while working alongside NGOs, local leaders, and academic institutions to co-create solutions tailored to real community needs.

A passionate mental health advocate, Escriva designs mentorship and safe learning spaces that integrate empathy with evidence-based approaches to wellness and resilience. He draws inspiration from thinkers like Dr. Jordan B. Peterson, applying principles of personal responsibility and growth to his professional and personal philosophy. Outside of work, Escriva is an artist, photographer, skateboarder, and cook; using creativity and movement to fuel innovation, balance, and self-expression. Looking ahead, he is focused on exploring how AI and digital health innovations can revolutionize community health and development, while bringing lessons from East Africa to a global stage. Through his career, mentorship, and advocacy, Escriva seeks to leave communities stronger, healthier, and more resilient, while inspiring others to pursue meaningful growth and impact.
